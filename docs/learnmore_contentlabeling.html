Enhancing cross-platform crisis detection and support

Improving online crisis detection with high-risk search queries

Koko is a mental health nonprofit that partners with social platforms to improve detection of harmful content and support for users at risk. Empowered by Koko's large database of 'high-risk' mental health search keywords, social platforms make informed trust and safety decisions tailored to meet their users' needs. To expand their content detection services in June 2022, I led Koko's content labeling efforts with two primary goals:

(1) Develop reliable, accessible labeling instructions for keyword updates
(2) Expand Koko's mental health keyword database of 'high-risk' search terms

Impact

  • Created clinical taxonomy of mental health search terms, expanding Koko's keyword database by 35%.
  • Led comprehensive audit of expanded mental health risk terms for TikTok, Pinterest, Google Search, and YouTube, exposing fundamental gaps in coverage (20-40%).
  • Generated report for presentation to heads of Trust and Safety, cross-functional teams of clinical directors and policy team members.
  • Developed risk terms database that is now integrated into key safety defenses for many large Internet platforms: Pinterest, Giphy, Tumblr, etc.

My Research

My research focuses on providing accessible, non-invasive, and affirming mental health support to young people experiencing thoughts of suicide or self-harm.
