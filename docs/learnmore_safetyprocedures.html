Ensuring safety of young research participants

Improving research safety for youth experiencing depression, suicidal thoughts, self-harm

The Lab for Scalable Mental Health is an academic research organization dedicated to improving access to evidence-based mental health supports among youth and young people. During their research participation, kids and teenagers often report having thoughts of suicide or recent experiences with self-harm. To ensure alignment with best practices in January 2020, I redesigned LSMH's safety procedures with three primary goals:

(1) Develop research safety procedures to align with scientific guidance
(2) Create training materials for staff members and supervise implementation
(3) Improve detection of suicide, self-harm to facilitate responsible intervention

Impact

  • Developed and implemented safety procedures across 4 virtual and in-person research studies (>900 participants) within 7 months.
  • Facilitated the creation of >50 personalized mental health support plans among youth experiencing thoughts of suicide or self-harm.

My Research

My research focuses on providing accessible, non-invasive, and affirming mental health support to young people experiencing thoughts of suicide or self-harm.
