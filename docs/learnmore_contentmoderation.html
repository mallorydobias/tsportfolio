Building a safe, trusted online peer-support community

Improving user safety within an online peer-support network

Koko is a mental health nonprofit that offers free, on-demand resources integrated within popular online platforms. Users who participate in Koko's anonymous peer support service can flag harmful or concerning content for internal review. To meet the needs of their growing user base in October 2022, I redesigned Koko's content moderation policies with three primary goals:

(1) Develop reliable, accessible instructions for future expert and non-expert content moderation workforces
(2) Improve detection and categorization of harmful and concerning content
(3) Present actionable crisis flow design recommendations for nonprofit co-founders

Impact

  • Refactored content moderation policies and instructions to reduce review time of flagged content by 10%.
  • Augmented moderation policies and instructions to account for new forms of abuse (e.g., 'sextortion').
  • Redesigned user flows to enhance safety and provide improved resources for users flagged as in distress.

My Research

My research focuses on providing accessible, non-invasive, and affirming mental health support to young people experiencing thoughts of suicide or self-harm.
